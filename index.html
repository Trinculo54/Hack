DarkPapers




DarkPapers is a tweak that adds over 50 OLED friendly and Full HD day/night wallpapers into the iOS 13 native dark mode wallpaper cycle (apperance aware). This tweak also comes with an app to let you manually add your own custom day/night wallpapers for you to use. No setting up is required, simply install the tweak and go to the wallpaper section in the settings app and look in the still wallpapers to see your new dark mode compatible wallpapers. Or, alternatively, go to the DarkPapers app to use your own custom wallpapers. This package depends on WallpaperLoader in order to work, which you can get in the repository:

https://skitty.xyz

All wallpapers included in this package are free to use. I do not own any rights above them and the credit goes to the original creators. The only changes made to these wallpapers by me were to tweak the darkness/color in some of them and to modify their original size.



This tweak is compatible with iOS 13 and above. The wallpapers included in the tweak are NOT optimized for iPads, only for iPhones. The DarkPapers app is compatible with iPads, so you can upload your own custom day/night wallpapers, but not use the ones that come with this tweak, due to their sizing.



Special thanks to skitty for the guide on how to add custom wallpapers into WallpaperLoader, and to MichaelMKenny for the awesome proof of concept app.

Compatibility:

Compatible with iOS 13 or later. Wallpapers from this package are not sized for iPads but iPad users can use the DarkPapers app to use their own custom wallpapers.

Dependencies:

WallpaperLoader and mobilesubstrate. You can get WallpaperLoader in the repository:

https://skitty.xyz

Known Issues:

When previewing the wallpapers in the native settings app, the wallpapers may sometimes appear zoomed in but they won’t be zoomed in once you apply the wallpaper. A way to trigger this bug is to enable/disable the native dark mode when previewing the wallpapers. To fix the bug, simply leave the preview and preview it again.
