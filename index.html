DarkPapers




DarkPapers is a tweak that adds over 50 OLED friendly and Full HD day/night wallpapers (with many more coming in future updates) into the iOS 13 native dark mode wallpaper switch (apperance aware feature). No setting up is required, simply install the tweak and go to the wallpaper section in the settings app and look in the still wallpapers to see your new dark mode compatible wallpapers. This package depends on WallpaperLoader in order to work, which you can get in the repository:

https://skitty.xyz/repo

All wallpapers included in this package are free to use. I do not own any rights above them and the credit goes to the original creators. The only changes made to these wallpapers by me were to tweak the darkness/color in some of them and to modify their original size.



This tweak is compatible with iOS 13 and above. The wallpapers included in the package are NOT sized for iPads, only for iPhones. iPad users can (optionally) downloads and use the tweak WallpaperLoader (Prefs) that is a different tweak from WallpaperLoader, in order to use their own custom wallpapers, or wait until the next update that adds iPad compatibility. Please note that due to limitations with GitHub I had to separate the wallpapers into packs, otherwise the binary would be corrupted by Git LFS due to the large size of the debian file. This means that you will install an additional 10 packs when installing DarkPapers, being the 10 packs the 50 wallpapers. This also means you can uninstall the packs you don't want/like as it won't conflict with any of the other packs nor DarkPapers. You can also individually install packs. If you uninstall WallpaperLoader, all the packs and DarkPapers will be uninstalled too.



Special thanks to Skittyblock for the guide on how to add custom wallpapers into WallpaperLoader.

Dependencies:

WallpaperLoader. You can get it at the repository:

https://skitty.xyz/repo

Known Issues:

When previewing the wallpapers in the native settings app, the wallpapers may sometimes appear zoomed in but they won’t be zoomed in once you apply the wallpaper. A way to trigger this bug is to enable/disable the native dark mode when previewing the wallpapers. To fix the bug, simply leave the preview and preview it again.
